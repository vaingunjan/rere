RECOMENDATIONS

The Promised Neverland

In many ways, The Promised Neverland feels like a much more focused version of Attack on Titan. Here, the trio of protagonists live in what appears to be an orphanage but is later revealed to be a farm, meant to raise human children and sell them to demons to consume. As such, they are likewise trapped in a place that's difficult to escape, facing the imminent threat of demons who want to eat them. Not only is the dark and suspenseful setting familiar, but so are the characters. Unwilling to give up without a fight, the kids are determined to escape, even without any assurance that a better future exists.

Tokyo Ghoul

Tokyo Ghoul, just like Attack on Titan, shows no hesitation to include dark moments and gore when necessary to drive the plot forward. Its world is brutal, and the stakes are high, constantly keeping viewers in suspense. While the titular ghouls also feed on the flesh of humans, the threat is not as obvious since they look just like humans and are able to hide among them. One such ghoul turns protagonist Kaneki into a ghoul-human hybrid, after which he tries to understand his new identity. Yet the world of ghouls is not an easy one to navigate, which is why he must adapt and grow if he wants to survive.

Seraph of the End: Vampire Reign

Much like Attack on Titan, Seraph of the End: Vampire Reign's story takes place in a world where humanity is on the brink of extinction (due to a virus killing everyone above the age of 13), with the remaining ones struggling to survive. Rather than being threatened by titans, humans here are subjugated by some of the most powerful vampires in anime. Yet when Yuichiro and Mikaela Hyakuya, as well as their orphanage family, try to flee from the vampires' rule, nearly everyone is killed. As a result, Yuichiro is determined to get his revenge against the ruthless vampires who took everything from him, seeking to save humans from their oppressors.

Parasyte: The Maxim

While Parasyte: The Maxim is generally darker and more mature, fans who enjoyed the brutal world of Attack on Titan will feel right at home watching the show. Of course, the gore scenes are not meant for pure shock value either, instead contributing to the overall intensity of the events and fear factor. Many traits of the protagonists and the presentation of monsters are quite familiar as well, as Parasyte's protagonist Shinichi is likewise involved with the enemy. After falling victim to one of the invading aliens, he has no choice but to struggle to survive while trying to uncover the truth.
